Zooming into the USA:
Prevalence of Short Sleep by County (2022)

Observations

There is far more variety in this data than was initially expected. There are some counties with less than 25% prevalence of short sleep and there are some with even more than 50%!

Further examination is necessary to understand exactly why there is so much variation. But this data could be utilized to target specific districts with the goal of further reinforcing the importance of sleep in early school curriculum.

It is also important to note that there are a couple of missing counties, signified by grey boxes. These counties' data was not present in the CDC PLACES dataset.

Note that darker reds imply higher prevalence of insufficient sleep. Insufficient sleep is defined as less than 7 hours of sleep per 24-hour period on average.
